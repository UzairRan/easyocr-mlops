Principles of chemotherapy  
The principles of chemotherapy involve the following key aspects:

Selectivity: Chemotherapy aims to selectively target and kill cancer cells while minimizing damage to healthy cells.

Combination therapy: Multiple chemotherapy drugs may be used together to increase effectiveness and reduce the risk of drug resistance.

Dose optimization: Chemotherapy doses are carefully calculated to achieve the maximum therapeutic effect while managing side effects.

Treatment cycles: Chemotherapy is often administered in cycles, allowing time for healthy cells to recover between doses.

Monitoring and adjustment: Treatment response is monitored through various tests, and chemotherapy regimens may be adjusted based on individual patient factors.

Supportive care: Measures are taken to manage and alleviate side effects of chemotherapy, such as nausea, fatigue, and immune system suppression.
